Here lie resources I have found useful, strange, or just unusually alive over the years.

> **A note (2026):** Link pages feel different now. Search engines answer, LLMs synthesize, and Perplexity has become extremely, well, *perplexive*. Still, there is something fun about a hand-built trail of links. It is not efficient. That is partly the point.
>
> Treat this page like a cabinet of curiosities, not a syllabus. Some links are practical, some are deep references, and some are here because they have the particular smell of an older internet where people built enormous pages because they felt like it.

If you want to look at a previous iteration, check out [my old website](https://sanathnu.github.io/Website-For-Cool-Websites/) where I linked to resources that excited me at the time. That repo is not maintained anymore, so some links may be broken.

## Start Here

- [The Missing Semester of Your CS Education](https://missing.csail.mit.edu/): The stuff universities assume you will somehow learn by osmosis: shells, editors, debugging, git, profiling.
- [Structure and Interpretation of Computer Programs](https://mitpress.mit.edu/sites/default/files/sicp/full-text/sicp/book/book.html): The classic wizard book for learning how programs can be thought about.
- [Security Engineering](https://www.cl.cam.ac.uk/~rja14/book.html): Ross Anderson's broad, opinionated book on dependable systems and security.
- [Refactoring Guru](https://refactoring.guru/): Design patterns and refactoring explained with visual clarity.
- [Build Your Own X](https://build-your-own-x.vercel.app/): Learn by rebuilding databases, games, operating systems, networks, and other beasts from scratch.
- [Project Based Learning](https://github.com/practical-tutorials/project-based-learning): A giant list of tutorials organized around making things.
- [No Debt CS](https://no-debt-cs.netlify.app/): A self-study computer science path without the tuition-shaped crater.

## Computer Science

### Programming, Systems, And Architecture

- [Linux From Scratch](https://www.linuxfromscratch.org/lfs/read.html): Build a Linux system piece by piece.
- [Linux Kernel Teaching](https://linux-kernel-labs.github.io/refs/heads/master/index.html): Labs and teaching material for kernel internals.
- [Linux Insides](https://0xax.gitbooks.io/linux-insides/content/): A guided walk through Linux internals.
- [Advanced Bash-Scripting Guide](https://tldp.org/LDP/abs/html/): Old-school, still useful, very internet.
- [Linux Network Administrators Guide](https://tldp.org/LDP/nag2/nag2.pdf): Network administration from the deep reference shelf.
- [Writing an OS in Rust](https://os.phil-opp.com/): A beautifully structured OS-from-scratch series.
- [The Little Book of Rust Books](https://lborb.github.io/book/): A map of Rust learning resources.
- [Rust Design Patterns](https://rust-unofficial.github.io/patterns/): Idioms, patterns, and anti-patterns in Rust.
- [The Hitchhiker's Guide to Python](https://docs.python-guide.org/): Practical Python conventions and ecosystem guidance.
- [Scipy Lecture Notes](https://scipy-lectures.org/index.html): Scientific Python, from NumPy to image processing.
- [Rosetta Code](https://rosettacode.org/wiki/Rosetta_Code): The same programming tasks implemented across many languages.
- [The Power of Prolog](https://www.metalevel.at/prolog): A sharp, surprisingly fun doorway into logic programming.
- [The Algorithms](https://the-algorithms.com/): Algorithms implemented across languages.
- [Algorithms by Jeff Erickson](http://jeffe.cs.illinois.edu/teaching/algorithms/#notes): Excellent free algorithms notes.
- [VisuAlgo](https://visualgo.net/en): Data structures and algorithms as animations.
- [CS 61B](https://sp21.datastructur.es/): Berkeley's data structures course.
- [CS 61C](https://inst.eecs.berkeley.edu/~cs61c/fa19/#lectures): Great material on machine structures and computer architecture.
- [Parallel Computer Architecture and Programming](http://15418.courses.cs.cmu.edu/spring2015/home): CMU's parallel systems course.
- [Computer Architecture History](https://people.cs.clemson.edu/~mark/hist.html): A historian's page for processor architecture.
- [Recent Processor Architects](https://people.cs.clemson.edu/~mark/architects.html): A useful people-map of architecture history.
- [FireSim Basics](https://docs.fires.im/en/latest/FireSim-Basics.html): FPGA-accelerated hardware simulation.
- [ZipCPU](https://zipcpu.com/): CPU design, formal methods, and hardware debugging rabbit holes.

### Cryptography, Security, And Privacy

- [Practical Cryptography for Developers](https://cryptobook.nakov.com/): A clear practical cryptography book.
- [Useful Cryptography Resources](https://blog.cryptographyengineering.com/useful-cryptography-resources/): Matthew Green's resource list.
- [Applied Cryptography Group](https://crypto.stanford.edu/): Stanford cryptography research group.
- [Cryptology ePrint Archive](https://eprint.iacr.org/): Preprints for modern cryptography.
- [Crypto Anarchy](https://cryptoanarchy.wiki/): A wiki around crypto-anarchist ideas and history.
- [Approachable Cryptography](https://cryptopedia.dev/#about): Friendly explanations of cryptographic constructions.
- [PicoCTF Primer](https://primer.picoctf.com/#_introduction): A beginner-friendly cybersecurity primer.
- [picoCTF](https://picoctf.org/): CMU's cybersecurity competition platform.
- [OpenSecurityTraining2](https://opensecuritytraining.info/Home.html): Serious low-level security training.
- [CyberChef](https://github.com/gchq/CyberChef): The Swiss army knife for encoding, decoding, compression, crypto, and forensic transformations.
- [OSINT Framework](https://osintframework.com/): A map of public-source intelligence tools.
- [Privacy Guides](https://www.privacyguides.org/): Privacy tools and practices without too much theater.
- [IntelTechniques Online Resources](https://inteltechniques.com/links.html): OSINT and privacy links from Michael Bazzell's ecosystem.
- [Free Haven's Anonymity Bibliography](https://www.freehaven.net/anonbib/topic.html): A deep bibliography on anonymity systems.
- [I2P](https://geti2p.net/en/): Anonymous network project.
- [Freenet Documentation](https://freenetproject.org/pages/documentation.html#install): Old, strange, and historically important decentralized publishing machinery.
- [Windows Sysinternals](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/sysinternals/): Essential Windows internals and troubleshooting tools.
- [Windows Internals Book](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/sysinternals/resources/windows-internals): Companion resources for the Windows Internals books.
- [TPM-JS](https://google.github.io/tpm-js/#pg_welcome): Learn TPM concepts in the browser.
- [Time-lock Encryption](https://gwern.net/self-decrypting): Gwern's notes on self-decrypting files and time-locked crypto.
- [research!rsc: Zip Files All The Way Down](https://research.swtch.com/zip): A wonderful example of software formats becoming haunted houses.

### Quantum Computing And Post-Quantum Crypto

- [Quantum Country](https://quantum.country/): Spaced-repetition quantum computing textbook by Andy Matuschak and Michael Nielsen.
- [Quantum Computing Since Democritus](https://www.scottaaronson.com/democritus/): Scott Aaronson's lecture notes on computation, physics, and complexity.
- [Preskill Physics 219](http://theory.caltech.edu/~preskill/ph229/): Quantum computation course material.
- [Quantum Algorithm Zoo](https://quantumalgorithmzoo.org/): A catalog of quantum algorithms.
- [Quantum Computing Learning Resources](https://qosf.org/learn_quantum/): QOSF's learning list.
- [Microsoft Quantum Katas](https://github.com/Microsoft/QuantumKatas): Quantum programming exercises.
- [Quirk](https://github.com/Strilanc/Quirk/wiki/How-to-use-Quirk): A delightful browser-based quantum circuit simulator.
- [pyQuil Quantum Virtual Machine](https://pyquil-docs.rigetti.com/en/v3.0.0/qvm.html): Documentation for Rigetti's simulator.
- [Quantiki](https://quantiki.org/): Quantum information portal and wiki.
- [Post-Quantum Cryptography at NIST](https://csrc.nist.gov/projects/post-quantum-cryptography/post-quantum-cryptography-standardization): NIST's PQC standardization project.
- [CRYSTALS](https://pq-crystals.org/index.shtml): Kyber and Dilithium project home.
- [Classic McEliece Talks](https://classic.mceliece.org/talks.html): Talks on a long-lived code-based cryptography candidate.
- [Kyber, How Does It Work?](https://cryptopedia.dev/posts/kyber/): A readable explanation of Kyber.
